# Child Compass 8.0 - Golden Conversation Benchmark

## Document Purpose
This document defines the permanent founder-approved benchmark suite used to evaluate Child Compass conversation quality at scale.

The benchmark exists to answer one release question:
Can Child Compass consistently deliver constitutional emotional quality across real parent scenarios without trust or hope regression?

This is a quality benchmark framework.

This is not implementation code.

This is not prompt design.

This is not conversation engine design.

---

## Benchmark Scope
The Golden Conversation Benchmark contains 500 founder-approved conversations.

Each conversation is evaluated using the Companion QA Engine scoring model.

Each category in this benchmark defines:
- purpose,
- success criteria,
- common failure modes,
- required emotional outcome.

---

## Benchmark Design Principles
1. Reality-first coverage
Benchmark conversations must represent lived parent reality, not idealized examples.

2. Emotional diversity
Conversations should include stress, confusion, conflict, joy, grief, and mixed emotions.

3. Capacity-aware scenarios
Cases must reflect low-energy and high-load states, not only reflective states.

4. Multi-turn relevance
At least part of each category should include multi-turn sequences, not single-turn prompts only.

5. Trust continuity
Scenarios should test whether quality holds over repeated interactions.

6. Hard-case inclusion
Every category should include edge cases where generic AI responses often fail.

## Category Specifications

### 1. Everyday Parenting (50)
Purpose:
Measure baseline companion quality in common daily friction and decision moments.

Success criteria:
- strong Presence and Recognition in ordinary strain,
- one realistic next step,
- stable trust tone without over-clinical framing.

Common failure modes:
- generic parenting clichés,
- advice lists,
- low-emotion utilitarian responses.

Required emotional outcome:
Parent feels understood, less alone, and capable of one practical action today.

### 2. PDA (20)
Purpose:
Evaluate autonomy-sensitive support quality under demand-related tension.

Success criteria:
- low-pressure language,
- choice-preserving action guidance,
- non-coercive framing.

Common failure modes:
- command-style advice,
- compliance framing,
- moral undertone around parent consistency.

Required emotional outcome:
Parent feels less trapped and more able to try one low-demand next step.

### 3. Autism (30)

- clinical over-explaining,
- abstract psychoeducation.

Required emotional outcome:
Parent feels emotionally validated and equipped with one realistic support move.

## Required Emotional Outcomes (Global)
Across all 500 benchmark conversations, successful quality means the parent reliably leaves feeling:
- understood,
- calmer,
- more capable,
- less alone,
- quietly hopeful.

If this emotional shift is not reliably observed, benchmark performance is insufficient regardless of technical quality.

---

## Benchmark Evaluation Method
Each benchmark conversation is scored using the Companion QA Engine rubric.

Per-conversation outputs:
- all category scores,
- Overall Companion Score,
- failure rule flags,
- pass or fail status,
- reviewer notes.

Per-category outputs:
- mean and median scores,
- floor-violation rate,
- hard-fail rate,
- common failure mode distribution,
- trust and hope trend signals.

Release-level outputs:
- global benchmark pass rate,
- critical category compliance,
- trust and hope regression status,
- launch recommendation.

---

## Benchmark Pass Rules
A release candidate passes the Golden Benchmark only if all conditions are true:
- Overall Companion Score across benchmark >= 8.2.
- Presence, Recognition, Emotional Timing, and Trust category means >= 8.0.
- Agency and Hope means >= 7.2.
- Hard-fail rate <= 1% overall.
- No category has hard-fail rate above 2%.
- No trust regression versus baseline.
- No hope regression versus baseline.

Additional stability rule:
At least 80% of categories must have no meaningful score regression against baseline.

---

## Regression Policy
Regression is defined against the most recent founder-approved baseline benchmark run.

Regression triggers:
- Trust mean drops by 0.2 or more.
- Hope mean drops by 0.2 or more.
- Emotional Timing mean drops by 0.2 or more.
- Any category hard-fail rate increases by 1.0 percentage point or more.

If any trigger occurs, release is blocked pending corrective iteration and full re-test.

---

## Benchmark Maintenance Policy
To keep the benchmark durable and representative:
- Category composition remains fixed at 500 total unless founder approval changes scope.
- Cases should be refreshed periodically while preserving category difficulty profile.
- Historical baselines must remain archived for regression comparisons.
- Any category revision requires documented rationale and founder sign-off.

Maintenance should preserve comparability over time.

---

## Launch Gate Integration
No future prompt, model, or conversation change may ship unless it:
- passes the Golden Benchmark,
- achieves required Companion Score thresholds,
- introduces no regression in Trust,
- introduces no regression in Hope.

If any criterion fails, launch is automatically blocked.

This benchmark is a constitutional release gate, not an advisory dashboard.

---

## Founder Decision Standard
The Golden Benchmark exists to protect parent trust under real conditions, not curated demos.

A release is acceptable only when benchmark evidence shows Child Compass can deliver consistent emotional safety, accurate recognition, practical agency, and grounded hope across the full lived spectrum of parenting scenarios.

That is the permanent benchmark standard.
